Project title: Term Deposit Subscription Prediction for Telemarketing Campaigns¶

1. Company Background:¶

This project analyses on a Portuguese bank that markets term deposits through telemarketing campaigns. As reaching out to customers one by one incurs expenses, it is crucial to enhance targeting. Only about 11–12% of clients subscribe, indicating that most calls do not yield value.The goal is to build a model that can identify potential customers more effectively.

1.1 Challenges faced by Businesses:¶

As I already witnessed in previous campaigns, an average of only 11% of people I have contacted signed up for a term deposit. As a result, most of the calling is done towards clients who convert the least. This wastes the call center, raises operating costs, and risks irritating clients with offers that are not valid on their purchases. The work I’m doing is aimed at helping the Bank reduce these wasted calls, increase the share of successful contacts, and ultimately improve the return on investment of its marketing campaigns.

1.2 Dataset¶

I will be using UCI Bank Marketing – full dataset (bank-full.csv) (Moro, Cortez and Rita, 2014) for this project. It has real campaign records of a Portuguese bank and it has both numerical and categorical features.

Dataset : https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/jiraleelas/uci-bank-marketing-full-dataset(Jiralee, 2024)

4.4 Preprocessing pipeline¶

In [86]:
numeric_pipeline = Pipeline([("imputer", SimpleImputer(strategy="median")),("scaler", StandardScaler())])
categorical_pipeline = Pipeline([("imputer", SimpleImputer(strategy="most_frequent")),("encoder", OneHotEncoder(drop="first",
                                                                                                                handle_unknown="ignore"))])
binned_pipeline = KBinsDiscretizer(n_bins=7,encode="ordinal",strategy="quantile")
preprocessor = ColumnTransformer(
    transformers=[
        ("num", numeric_pipeline, numeric_feats),
        ("cat", categorical_pipeline, low_card_cat + high_card_cat),
        ("bin", binned_pipeline, binned_feats),
    ],
    remainder="drop")

x_train_pro = preprocessor.fit_transform(X_train)

6.4 Prediction Acceptance Rate:¶

  • A threshold is determined (such as calling clients who have a predicted P(yes) of at least 0.4), and acceptance rate is calculated for those selected. If that rate is much higher than the overall 11%, the model is targeting high probability customers.

6.5 Why does Accuracy looks high?¶

  • In this case only 11% or so of customers subscribed, therefore creating an extremely unbalanced classification problem, thus only assessing accuracy can be misleading, even a simple no prediction will do well.(Moro, Cortez and Rita, 2014)
  • Effectiveness of this Model : Even so, the model performs well by detecting a large number of true subscribers while still performing well on the majority class, making it more useful than a simple baseline.

6.6 Final Model Selection:¶

  • The SMOTE + Random Forest model balances the outcomes well between the classes and enhance identification of potential subscribers. The bank can focus on high-probability customers, alter thresholds as per allocated budget, and enhance campaign effectiveness.

    ## Why selected model is best?

  • This model was chosen as it scored well on both classes with a higher macro F1 and a strong ROC-AUC. By limiting contacts to customers most likely to subscribe, it reduces dialling efforts and improves campaign results.

7. Conclusion¶

7.1 Business Recommendations¶

  • Concentrate on making calls to customers who have a high likelihood of acceptance and avoid the callers with very low scores.
  • Adjust that as needed. Incorporate the model in the campaign process to aid targeting with data.
  • Within the CRM system, real-time scoring of customers can be executed through use of the tool.
  • Define the decision threshold according to budget and call-centre capacity.

7.2 Key Insights from the model:¶

  • The subscription likelihood of some customers varies with their age, job, last contact, and duration of call. Moro, Cortez and Rita (2014) mention that the patterns improve targeting and success. In addition, it allows the bank to concentrate efforts on customers with a higher response likelihood.

7.3 Learning Outcomes¶

  • Utilizing SMOTE enhances the model’s capacity to detect “Yes” cases. Random Forest does a good job here as it can deal with mixed features. As positive cases are uncommon, metrics such as macro F1 and ROC-AUC allow for a more realistic assessment of performance than accuracy.

7.4 Most Impacting Hyperparameters¶

  • The amount of trees helps determine the stability of the model. More trees generally lead to better performance. By regulating the depth of the tree, the learning algorithm limits complexity to avoid overfitting.
  • Employing 200 trees with a depth of 10 is a good combination in this case as it allows the model to learn useful patterns without being too complex.

7.6 Limitations of the Model¶

  • The model is based on data from past campaigns, so it might not work as well if customers change how they act over time. Some features, like call duration, are only available after the call starts, which makes it hard to use decision-making before the call. SMOTE adds synthetic samples, which might also have noise from the original data.
  • Also, the model doesn't take into account the costs of running a business, so it should only be used as a decision support tool and not as a stand-alone system.
