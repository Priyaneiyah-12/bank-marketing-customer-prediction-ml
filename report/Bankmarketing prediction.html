Project title: Term Deposit Subscription Prediction for Telemarketing Campaigns 1. Company Background: This project analyses on a Portuguese bank that markets term deposits through telemarketing campaigns. As reaching out to customers one by one incurs expenses, it is crucial to enhance targeting. Only about 11–12% of clients subscribe, indicating that most calls do not yield value.The goal is to build a model that can identify potential customers more effectively. 1.1 Challenges faced by Businesses: As I already witnessed in previous campaigns, an average of only 11% of people I have contacted signed up for a term deposit. As a result, most of the calling is done towards clients who convert the least. This wastes the call center, raises operating costs, and risks irritating clients with offers that are not valid on their purchases. The work I’m doing is aimed at helping the Bank reduce these wasted calls, increase the share of successful contacts, and ultimately improve the return on investment of its marketing campaigns. 1.2 Dataset I will be using UCI Bank Marketing – full dataset (bank-full.csv) (Moro, Cortez and Rita, 2014) for this project. It has real campaign records of a Portuguese bank and it has both numerical and categorical features. Dataset : https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/jiraleelas/uci-bank-marketing-full-dataset(Jiralee, 2024) 2. The best model achieved a CV F1 of ~0.73 with 200 trees and a depth of 10 because further learning would not be able to take place and a case of overfitting would occur (Moro, Cortez and Rita, 2014). 5.2 Imbalance handling: SMOTE + Random Forest smote_balancer = SMOTE(sampling_strategy="auto",k_neighbors=5,random_state=40) rf_smote_model = RandomForestClassifier(n_estimators=200,max_depth=10,random_state=40,n_jobs=-1) smote_rf_pipeline = ImbPipeline(steps=[("smote", smote_balancer),("rf", rf_smote_model),]) cv_scores_smote = cross_val_score(smote_rf_pipeline,x_train_proc,y_train,cv=5,scoring="f1_macro",n_jobs=-1) print( f"SMOTE + RF | mean F1_macro: {cv_scores_smote.mean():.4f} " f"(± {2 * cv_scores_smote.std():.4f})" ) smote_rf_pipeline.fit(X_train_proc, y_train) SMOTE + RF | mean F1_macro: 0.7425 (± 0.0070) Pipeline i SMOTE RandomForestClassifier ?
